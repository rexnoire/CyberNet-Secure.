Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our cybersecurity services and solutions

What is cybersecurity and why is it important?

Cybersecurity refers to the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. These attacks are usually aimed at accessing, changing, or destroying sensitive information; extorting money from users; or interrupting normal business processes.

Implementing effective cybersecurity measures is particularly challenging today because there are more devices than people, and attackers are becoming more innovative.

How do I know if my business needs cybersecurity services?

Every business that uses digital technology needs cybersecurity. Specifically, you should consider professional cybersecurity services if:

  • You handle sensitive customer data
  • You process online payments
  • Your employees work remotely
  • You use cloud services
  • You haven't had a security assessment in the past year
  • You're subject to compliance regulations (GDPR, HIPAA, PCI-DSS, etc.)

How quickly can you respond to a security incident?

Our emergency response team is available 24/7 and can typically begin addressing critical security incidents within 30 minutes of notification. Response time may vary based on the severity of the incident and your service level agreement.

We follow a structured incident response process that includes containment, eradication, recovery, and post-incident analysis to prevent future occurrences.

What is included in your penetration testing service?

Our comprehensive penetration testing service includes:

  • Pre-engagement interactions and scope definition
  • Intelligence gathering and reconnaissance
  • Vulnerability analysis and exploitation
  • Post-exploitation and privilege escalation
  • Detailed reporting with risk ratings
  • Remediation guidance and verification testing

We test web applications, network infrastructure, wireless networks, and social engineering vectors based on your needs.

Can you help with compliance requirements like GDPR or HIPAA?

Absolutely. We have extensive experience helping organizations meet various compliance requirements including:

  • GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
  • HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)

We can conduct gap assessments, implement necessary controls, and provide documentation for audits.
